# Data-Science-Salaries
An exploratory data analysis project on data science salaries. The dataset is analyzed to identify salary trends based on experience level, job title, company size, and location. Visualizations are used to make insights clear and easy to understand.
Dataset Overview

work_year – The year in which the compensation was received

experience_level – Professional level such as entry-level, mid-level, senior, or executive

employment_type – Nature of employment including full-time, part-time, contract, or freelance

job_title – Designation or role in the data field

salary – Annual pay expressed in USD

employee_residence – Country where the employee is based

company_location – Country where the employer operates

company_size – Organization scale categorized as small, medium, or large

job_type – Work arrangement such as remote, hybrid, or on-site

Key Findings

The salary prediction model performs with satisfactory accuracy.

Professional experience has the highest impact on salary levels.

Factors like company size and work arrangement also play a notable role.

Conclusion

The data science industry presents strong earning potential, particularly for experienced professionals and remote roles. Compensation is shaped by a combination of elements including experience level, employment structure, organization size, and geographical location. This study offers valuable insights into global salary trends and can help both professionals and organizations make informed, data-driven decisions.
